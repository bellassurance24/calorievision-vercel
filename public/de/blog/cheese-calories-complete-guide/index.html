Cheese Calories Decoded: From Cheddar to Brie and Everything Between

Cheese often sparks debate in nutrition. People praise it for its protein and calcium, but also worry about its calories and saturated fat. The reality is more balanced. Knowing how many calories are in cheese can help you enjoy it without hurting your health goals. Let’s clear up the confusion and see what’s really in your favorite cheeses.

The General Rule: Cheese Is Calorie-Dense

Most cheeses have 80 to 120 calories per one-ounce serving (about 28 grams). This makes cheese a calorie-dense food, mainly because of its high fat content.

A typical pre-packaged cheese slice weighs about one ounce and has 80 to 110 calories. Restaurant servings and cheese boards usually offer much more, sometimes adding up to several hundred calories.

Cheese is calorie-dense because of how it’s made. As milk turns into cheese, water is removed, and fat and protein become more concentrated. This process leaves cheese rich in nutrients and calories.

One cup of shredded cheese contains approximately 400-450 calories, a quantity easily exceeded when topping nachos, pizzas, or casseroles. This serving size helps contextualize how cheese calories accumulate in everyday cooking.

Popular Cheeses and Their Calories

Cheese types vary in calories, depending on their fat and moisture content.

Cheddar cheese has about 113 calories per ounce. It’s popular for its taste and nutrition, offering around 7 grams of protein per serving. Sharp and mild cheddar have the same calories; the only difference is how long they are aged.

Mozzarella cheese calories depend on the type. Whole milk mozzarella has about 85 calories per ounce, while part-skim has around 72. Fresh mozzarella balls have similar calorie counts to whole-milk mozzarella.

Parmesan cheese is surprisingly calorie-dense at about 111 calories per ounce. However, its intense flavor means smaller amounts satisfy, potentially reducing actual calorie impact. A tablespoon of grated Parmesan contains approximately 22 calories.

Swiss cheese has about 108 calories per ounce. The holes come from bacteria making carbon dioxide during fermentation, but these holes don’t really change the calorie count.

Brie cheese contains approximately 95 calories per ounce. Despite its creamy, rich texture, brie is actually lower in calories than harder cheeses because of its higher moisture content.

Gouda cheese has about 101 calories per ounce. Aged Gouda has slightly more calories than young Gouda because it loses moisture as it ages.

Feta cheese is lighter, with about 75 calories per ounce. Its crumbly texture and strong flavor mean you can use less and still enjoy the taste.

Blue cheese has about 100 calories per ounce. Other types like Gorgonzola, Roquefort, and Stilton have similar calorie counts, even though their flavors differ.

Cream cheese is high in calories, with about 99 per ounce, mostly from fat. One tablespoon of cream cheese has about 50 calories.

American cheesAmerican cheese, which is processed, has about 94 calories per ounce. Individually wrapped slices usually have 70 to 80 calories each because they are a bit smaller. These Options

If you’re watching your calories, some cheeses have fewer calories than others.

Cottage cheese stands apart from other cheeses, with only 25-30 calories per ounce in low-fat varieties. One cup of low-fat cottage cheese contains approximately 160 calories and 28 grams of protein, making it an excellent choice for those seeking a calorie-conscious source of protein.

Part-skim ricotta cheese has about 50 calories per ounce. Its mild taste and creamy texture make it good for both sweet and savory dishes.

Reduced-fat cheeses usually have 25 to 50 percent fewer calories than regular versions. For example, reduced-fat cheddar may have 80 calories per ounce instead of 113. The taste and texture may not be as good, but you do save calories.

Laughing Cow and similar spreadable cheese wedges have 35 to 50 calories each, and the packaging helps with portion control.

String cheese is easy to portion, with about 80 calories per stick. The individual packaging helps you avoid eating too much, which can happen with block cheese.

Goat cheese (chèvre) contains about 75 calories per ounce. This is a bit less than many cow’s milk cheeses, and it has a unique tangy flavor.

The problem comes when you eat cheese without measuring.
