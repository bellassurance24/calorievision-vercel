How Many Calories in Eggs? Complete Nutrition Guide

Eggs are a versatile and nutritious food. No matter how you like them—scrambled, hard-boiled, or poached—knowing their calorie content can help you make better choices for your diet.

This guide covers how many calories are in eggs cooked in different ways, explains their nutritional benefits, and offers tips for adding eggs to a healthy diet.

Basic Egg Calories

A large egg (about 50 grams) has about 70-78 calories. The exact number depends on the egg’s size and how you cook it.

A small egg has about 54 calories, a medium egg has around 63, and a large egg (the usual grocery store size) has 70-78. Extra-large eggs have about 80 calories, and jumbo eggs can have up to 90.

The majority of an egg's calories come from the yolk. The egg white contains only about 17 calories, while the yolk contains approximately 55 calories. This is why many people who are watching their calorie intake choose to eat only egg whites.

Calories by Cooking Method

The way you cook eggs changes their calorie count. Here’s a look at the most common cooking methods.

Boiled Eggs (Hard or Soft)

Boiled eggs are low in calories since you don’t need to add fat to cook them. A large hard- or soft-boiled egg has about 78 calories. Hard-boiled eggs are also great for meal prep and can be kept in the fridge for up to a week.

Poached Eggs

Poached eggs are also low in calories, with about 71-78 calories per large egg. Like boiling, poaching doesn’t need added fat—just simmering water and a little vinegar. Poached eggs are often used in dishes like Eggs Benedict, but keep in mind the sauce adds extra calories.

Fried Eggs

Fried eggs have more calories than boiled or poached eggs because they’re cooked in fat. A large fried egg made with one teaspoon of butter has about 115 calories. Using a tablespoon of butter raises it to about 150. If you use cooking spray instead, fried eggs can be closer to 90 calories.

A sunny-side-up egg typically has fewer calories than an over-easy or over-hard egg because it's only cooked on one side, requiring less cooking fat. However, the difference is minimal if you use the same amount of oil.

Scrambled Eggs

Scrambled eggs can have different calorie counts based on what you add. Plain scrambled eggs with just butter have about 90-100 calories per large egg. Adding a tablespoon of whole milk adds about 9 calories, while a tablespoon of heavy cream adds about 52.

Scrambled eggs at restaurants often have 150-200 calories per egg because they’re made with more butter and cream for a richer taste.

Omelettes

A basic two-egg omelette cooked in butter contains approximately 190-220 calories before any fillings. However, omelettes rarely come plain. Adding cheese increases the calorie count by 80-120 calories per ounce. Vegetables like peppers, onions, and mushrooms add minimal calories (about 10-20 total), while meat fillings like ham, bacon, or sausage can add 50-150 calories.

A restaurant three-egg omelette with cheese, ham, and vegetables can have 500-700 calories or more.

Baked Eggs

Baked eggs, or shirred eggs, have about 90-120 calories each, depending on whether you add butter or cream. Shakshuka, a Middle Eastern dish with eggs baked in tomato sauce, has about 150-200 calories per egg, including the sauce.

Egg White vs Whole Egg

Many people who are watching calories or focused on fitness choose egg whites instead of whole eggs. Here’s a comparison.

One large egg white contains only 17 calories, 3.6 grams of protein, and zero fat. In contrast, one large whole egg contains 78 calories, 6 grams of protein, and 5 grams of fat. The yolk contains all of the egg's fat, cholesterol, and most of its vitamins. Egg whites are much lower in calories, but whole eggs have more nutrients. The yolk has vitamins A, D, E, and K, B vitamins, iron, and choline. Choline is especially important for brain health and is hard to find in other foods.

For most people, eating 1-3 whole eggs a day is healthy and gives you better nutrition than eating only egg whites.

Popular Egg Dishes and Their Calories

Knowing how many calories are in different egg dishes can help you make better choices when eating out or cooking at home.

Breakfast Classics

A classic breakfast of two fried eggs, two strips of bacon, and two slices of buttered toast contains approximately 500-600 calories. Eggs Benedict with Canadian bacon and hollandaise sauce on an English muffin typically has 550-700 calories per serving. A breakfast burrito with scrambled eggs, cheese, beans, and salsa ranges from 400 to 800 calories, depending on the size and ingredients.

Egg Sandwiches

A simple egg-and-cheese sandwich on an English muffin has about 300-350 calories. Fast food versions vary: a McDonald's Egg McMuffin has 310 calories, while a Starbucks bacon and egg sandwich has 500. Making sandwiches at home lets you control ingredients and usually means fewer calories.

Quiche and Frittata

A slice of quiche Lorraine (bacon and cheese) contains approximately 350-450 calories due to the buttery crust and cream filling. A crustless quiche or frittata is a lower-calorie alternative, at about 200-300 calories per serving, depending on the fillings.

Deviled Eggs

Deviled eggs have about 60-80 calories per half, depending on how much mayo is in the filling. Four halves add up to 240-320 calories. Using Greek yogurt instead of mayo can cut calories by about 30%.

Egg Salad

A half-cup serving of traditional egg salad made with mayonnaise contains approximately 250-350 calories. This can vary significantly based on how much mayo is used. Lighter versions made with Greek yogurt or avocado instead of mayo contain about 150-200 calories per half cup.

Eggs at Popular Restaurants

Restaurant egg dishes often contain more calories than homemade versions due to larger portions and more cooking fat.

Fast Food Breakfast

Most fast food breakfast sandwiches containing eggs range from 300 to 600 calories. Breakfast platters with eggs, bacon, pancakes, and hash browns can contain 800-1,200 calories or more. If you're watching calories at fast food restaurants, opt for simple egg sandwiches without extra bacon or sausage.

Diners and Breakfast Restaurants

Diner breakfasts are often high in calories. A Grand Slam-style meal with eggs, bacon, sausage, pancakes, and hash browns can go over 1,500 calories. IHOP omelettes range from 500 to 1,200 calories, and Denny’s egg breakfasts usually have 600-1,000.

At breakfast restaurants, order eggs cooked simply, like poached or hard-boiled. Skip butter on toast and pick fruit instead of hash browns.

Hotel Breakfast Buffets

Buffet scrambled eggs usually have cream and butter, with about 150-200 calories per scoop. It’s easy to overeat at hotel buffets, so watch your portions. Choose boiled eggs if you can—they’re always lower in calories.

Nutritional Benefits of Eggs

Eggs aren’t just low in calories—they also have great nutritional benefits, making them a good choice for most diets.

Protein Content

Eggs are an excellent source of complete protein, meaning they contain all nine essential amino acids your body needs. One large egg provides 6 grams of high-quality protein, with about 60% in the white and 40% in the yolk. This makes eggs ideal for muscle building, weight management, and satiety.

Healthy Fats

Despite old concerns about dietary cholesterol, research now shows that eggs can be part of a heart-healthy diet for most people. The fats in eggs are mostly unsaturated, and eggs contain omega-3 fatty acids, especially if they come from pasture-raised hens.

Vitamins and Minerals

Eggs are one of the few natural food sources of vitamin D. They also provide significant amounts of vitamin B12, riboflavin, and selenium. The yolk contains choline, a nutrient essential for brain function that most people don't get enough of.

Eye Health

Eggs have lutein and zeaxanthin, antioxidants that build up in your eyes and help protect against macular degeneration and cataracts as you age.

Tips for Reducing Egg Dish Calories

If you enjoy eggs but want to eat fewer calories, try these practical tips.

Use Cooking Spray Instead of Butter

Using cooking spray instead of a tablespoon of butter saves about 100 calories per serving. Non-stick pans also help you use less fat when making eggs.

Mix Whole Eggs with Egg Whites

InsteadFor an omelette, try using one whole egg and two egg whites instead of three whole eggs. This cuts calories from about 234 to 146 but still gives you the taste and texture of the yolk. Your Fillings Wisely

Fill omelettes and scrambled eggs with vegetables like spinach, tomatoes, peppers, and mushrooms instead of cheese and meat. This adds more food and nutrients without many extra calories.

Skip the Cheese or Use Less

Cheese is often the biggest calorie contributor to egg dishes. Using 1 tablespoon of shredded cheese instead of 1/4 cup saves about 80 calories while still providing the same amount of cheese flavor.

Order Poached or Hard-Boiled When Eating Out

Restaurant scrambled eggs are almost always cooked with more fat than you'd use at home. Poached and hard-boiled eggs are consistent regardless of who prepares them.

How Many Eggs Can You Eat Per Day?

For most healthy adults, eating 1-3 whole eggs per day is safe and nutritious. Studies have shown that this level of egg consumption does not increase the risk of heart disease in healthy people.

Athletes and people trying to build muscle often eat more eggs for their protein content. Some bodybuilders consume 4-6 eggs daily, though they may separate some yolks to manage fat intake.

If you have diabetes, high cholesterol, or heart disease, talk to your doctor about how many eggs are right for you, since people respond differently to dietary cholesterol.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are brown eggs healthier than white eggs?

No, the color of the eggshell has no impact on nutrition or calories. Brown eggs come from different breeds of hens than white eggs, but the nutritional content is identical.

Do organic eggs have fewer calories?

Organic eggs have the same calories as regular eggs. They might have a bit more omega-3 if the hens had access to pasture.

Are eggs bad for cholesterol?

For most people, no. Recent research has shown that dietary cholesterol has less impact on blood cholesterol than previously thought. The bigger factors are saturated fat intake and overall diet quality.

What's the healthiest way to eat eggs?

Poached or hard-boiled eggs are the lowest-calorie options since they require no added fat. However, any cooking method can be part of a healthy diet when consumed in moderation.

Can I eat eggs every day?

Yes, most healthy adults can eat eggs every day as part of a balanced diet. Studies show that eating up to three eggs a day doesn’t raise disease risk.
