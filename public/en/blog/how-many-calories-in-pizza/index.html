Tips for Reducing Pizza Calories

Choose thin crust over thick, pan, Choose thin crust over thick, pan, or stuffed crust to save 80-150 calories per slice. Ask for light or half cheese to reduce calories by 40-60 per slice. Load up on vegetable toppings like mushrooms, peppers, onions, and spinach. Skip meat or choose lean options like grilled chicken instead of pepperoni or sausage. Avoid stuffed-crust and cheese-filled options. Choose tomato-based sauce over creamy white or Alfredo sauces. Blot excess oil with a napkin to remove 20-40 calories per slice. Frequently Asked Questions

How many calories are in a whole pizza?

A whole large cheese pizza (14 inches, eight slices) contains approximately 1,800-2,400 calories, depending on the crust type and toppings.

Is pizza healthier than a burger?

It depends on portions. Two slices of cheese pizza (about 500 calories) are comparable to a regular cheeseburger. However, pizza provides more calcium from cheese, while burgers offer more protein.

Can I eat pizza on a diet?

Yes! Choose thin crust, load up on veggies, go light on cheese, and limit yourself to 1-2 slices. Pair with a side salad instead of breadsticks or wings.

What's the lowest-calorie pizza at Domino's?

The Pacific Veggie on thin crust is one of the lowest options at approximately 180 calories per slice.

How many calories are in a Costco pizza slice?

Costco pizza slices are notably large, containing approximately 700-720 calories per slice, which is equivalent to 2-3 regular pizza slices.

Is cauliflower-crust pizza actually healthier?

Cauliflower crust typically has 30-50% fewer carbs and calories than regular crust. However, some store-bought versions add extra ingredients that increase calories, so always check the label.
