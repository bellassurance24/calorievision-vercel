How Many Calories in a Burger? Complete Guide

Burgers are among the most popular foods worldwide. Whether you grab a quick meal at a fast-food chain, dine at a gourmet restaurant, or grill at home, knowing your burger's calorie content helps you make informed dietary choices.

In this guide, we'll break down how many calories are in different types of burgers, compare popular restaurant options, and share tips for enjoying burgers while managing your calorie intake.

Average Calories in a Burger

Burger calories can vary dramatically depending on size, ingredients, and preparation method. Here's a general overview:

  • Simple homemade burger: 250-400 calories

  • Standard fast-food burger: 250-550 calories

  • Double cheeseburger: 400-700 calories

  • Gourmet restaurant burger: 600-1,200 calories

  • Loaded specialty burger: 1,000-1,500+ calories

The average burger in the United States contains about 400-600 calories, but this can easily double or triple with certain toppings and sides.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many calories are in a plain hamburger?

A plain hamburger with just a bun and patty (no cheese or toppings) contains approximately 250-350 calories, depending on the size of the patty and type of bun.

How many calories are in a McDonald's Big Mac?

A McDonald's Big Mac contains 550 calories. This includes two beef patties, special sauce, lettuce, cheese, pickles, onions, and a sesame seed bun.

What is the lowest-calorie fast food burger?

Most fast-food chains offer basic hamburgers around 240-280 calories. McDonald's Hamburger (250 cal), Burger King Hamburger (240 cal), and Wendy's Jr. Hamburger (250 cal) are among the lowest options. For even fewer calories, ask for no bun or a lettuce wrap.

Are turkey burgers healthier than beef?

Turkey burgers are generally lower in calories and saturated fat than beef burgers. A 4-oz turkey patty has about 170 calories compared to 280+ for beef. However, "healthier" depends on your overall diet and nutritional goals.

How can I estimate burger calories when eating out?

Look for nutrition information on the restaurant's website or app. If unavailable, estimate based on size and toppings. Use a tool like CalorieVision to snap a photo and get an AI-powered estimate. When in doubt, assume restaurant burgers contain 600-900 calories.

Do toppings really add that many calories?

Yes! Toppings can add 200-500+ calories to a burger. Cheese adds 70-110, bacon adds 80-100, mayo adds 90-100 per tablespoon, and avocado adds 50-60. Fried toppings like onion rings can add 100-150 calories. Stick to vegetables and mustard for low-calorie toppings.
