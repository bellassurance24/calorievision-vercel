## PRODUCT ANALYSIS

### Value Proposition
AI-powered meal scanner that gives you instant calories, macros, and full nutrition data just by pointing your phone at any food. No logging. No searching databases. Just snap and know.

### Key Features
- Instant AI photo-based calorie & macro estimation
- Full macronutrient breakdown (protein, carbs, fat, fiber)
- Works on any meal — restaurant, homemade, packaged
- 100% free — no subscription required
- Privacy-first (no data stored)
- Multilingual (8 languages)

### Current CTAs
- "Start analyzing your meals today"
- Main URL: calorievision.online

### Trust Signals
- Free to use (zero cost barrier)
- Privacy-focused (no data retention)
- Professional, educational positioning
- Clean, fast UX

### PERSONA A — "The Determined Dieter"
**Tag:** "She's tried everything. This time, she means it."

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Age | 25–45 |
| Gender | Primarily female (75% F / 25% M) |
| Income | $35,000–$75,000/year |
| Location | Suburban USA — Midwest, South, Southeast |
| Education | Some college to bachelor's degree |
| Family Status | Married with kids OR single professional |
| Device | Mobile-first (iPhone or Android) |

**Psychographics:**
- Wants to lose 20–50 lbs but feels overwhelmed by calorie counting
- Has tried MyFitnessPal, Weight Watchers, Noom — struggles with consistency
- Feels guilty after eating out because she "doesn't know the calories"
- Motivated by upcoming events: wedding, vacation, reunion
- Follows weight loss accounts on Instagram and TikTok
- Responds to transformation stories and "real women" content
- Values convenience above all — no complicated systems

**Top Pain Points:**
1. **Calorie counting is a full-time job** — searching every food in a database kills momentum
2. **Restaurant meals are a mystery** — she avoids eating out because she can't track it
3. **She loses progress and gives up** — the friction of logging makes her stop after 2 weeks
4. **She doesn't trust herself** — portion sizes confuse her; she over or under-estimates
5. **"Healthy" food still makes her gain weight** — she doesn't understand macros

**Buying Triggers:**
- Sees a before/after transformation on social media
- Just had a "bad eating" weekend and wants to reset
- Upcoming event forces her to get serious (wedding, summer, etc.)
- Friend or influencer recommends a nutrition tool
- Frustration with manual calorie logging boils over

**Meta Targeting — Persona A:**
- **Interests:** Weight loss, Calorie counting, Dieting, Intermittent fasting, Healthy eating, MyFitnessPal, Weight Watchers, Noom, Clean eating, Meal prep
- **Behaviors:** Health & wellness enthusiasts, Mobile shoppers, Engaged shoppers
- **Age:** 25–45 | **Gender:** All | **Country:** US
